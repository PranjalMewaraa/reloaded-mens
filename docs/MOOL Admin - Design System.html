01 — Brief

A utilitarian admin,
tuned for the counter, not the desk.

Ops-critical screens (stock decrement, today's orders, return verification, lead handoff) are designed at 375px first — they happen on a phone or tablet at the store counter. Configuration screens (product edit, promotions, analytics, settings) are designed at 1440px first, with usable mobile fallbacks. Shared tokens with the storefront; admin-specific components on top. Tone: utilitarian, fast, calm. No marketing motion. No decorative photography. Generous tap targets (48×48). Big numbers, dense lists.

Users
Owner (Admin) · Counter staff (Staff)
Primary device

Why this matters

→ Search is sticky to the top, but every −1 button is in the lower 2/3 of the screen so a phone held in one hand reaches them with the thumb without shifting grip.

→ Tap = −1 (most common). Long-press = quantity sheet. Both feel identical for the first touch — no surprises.

→ Toast persists 60s and is always undo-able. After it dismisses, the decrement is committed to the audit log permanently (with timestamp, user, reason).

→ Low-stock rows tint warning-yellow; OOS rows dim and swap the −1 button for a "Restock" CTA — staff can never accidentally decrement past zero.
